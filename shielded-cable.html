Shielded Cable EMI Problem

A real-world electromagnetic interference issue

üßæ Problem Description

Electromagnetic Interference (EMI) is a common threat to network signal quality. It occurs when electromagnetic radiation from external sources‚Äîsuch as lightning strikes, nearby power lines, industrial motors, UPS units, transformers, or even weather conditions like heavy rain‚Äîinterferes with data transmission.

To combat these threats, Shielded Twisted Pair (STP) cables are often used instead of Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP). STP cables include a metallic shield that protects the data lines from external noise and interference.

STP cables offer protection not only against man-made EMI sources but also against natural interference such as:

  • Lightning discharges near outdoor cabling
  • Heavy rain causing electromagnetic noise on aerial lines
  • Magnetic fields from nearby power stations or transformers
  • Cross-frequency interference from adjacent radio/microwave links

This makes STP especially suitable for high-interference environments such as factories, hospitals, military installations, and data centers with sensitive electronics.

However, STP cables must be properly installed and grounded. If not:

  • The shielding can act as an antenna and collect EMI instead of blocking it.
  • Improper grounding (e.g., at both ends or no end) can create ground loops, introducing more noise.
  • Non-metallic RJ45 connectors may break the shielding continuity.
  • The shielding may not be connected to the device chassis (e.g., switch, NIC), making it useless.

These issues can lead to:

  • CRC (Cyclic Redundancy Check) errors on interfaces
  • Late collisions in half-duplex systems
  • Random disconnections and retransmissions
  • Overall degraded throughput despite good hardware

  • Use STP only when EMI sources are present; otherwise, UTP is simpler and more cost-effective.
  • Ground the cable shield at one end only (preferably patch panel side) to prevent ground loops.
  • Ensure devices (switches, NICs) support shielded connections and are bonded to ground.
  • Use proper shielded RJ45 connectors and ensure full 360¬∞ shielding contact.
  • Route STP cables away from high EMI sources (minimum 30 cm distance from power lines, UPS units).
  • Use commands like show interfaces to check for CRC, input errors, or collisions.
